💊 Medicine Guide

Common Pet Medicines

General information on widely used over-the-counter and vet-prescribed medicines. Always verify with your vet before use.

💊

Antiparasitic Treatments

Commonly used for fleas, ticks, and worms. Fipronil (Frontline), Ivermectin, and Praziquantel are widely recommended. Dosage is strictly weight-based.

⚠ Never use dog flea treatments on cats
🧴

Skin & Allergy Relief

Chlorphenamine (antihistamine) helps with mild allergies. Hydrocortisone cream (0.5–1%) can soothe localised irritation. Oatmeal shampoo is safe for most pets.

⚠ Avoid prolonged steroid use without vet guidance
🫀

Digestive Aids

Probiotics (FortiFlora, Proviable) restore gut flora after diarrhea. Metronidazole is commonly prescribed for GI infections. Kaolin-pectin helps mild stomach upsets.

⚠ Bland diet (rice + chicken) aids most GI issues
👁

Eye & Ear Drops

Saline eye wash is safe for gentle cleaning. Chloramphenicol eye drops treat bacterial conjunctivitis. Clotrimazole ear drops address fungal ear infections.

⚠ Don't use human eye drops — pH differs
🦴

Pain & Joint Support

Carprofen and Meloxicam are vet-prescribed NSAIDs for dogs. Glucosamine + Chondroitin supplements support joint health in senior pets.

⚠ Never give ibuprofen or paracetamol to pets
🌡️

Fever & Infection

Amoxicillin and Cephalexin are common vet-prescribed antibiotics. Never self-prescribe antibiotics — incorrect use causes resistance. Report fever above 39.5°C to a vet.

⚠ Antibiotics require vet prescription
🚨 Emergency Signs

When to See a Vet Immediately

Some situations go beyond home advice. Recognise these red-flag signs and act fast.

🩸
Bleeding / Wounds

Any uncontrolled bleeding, deep cuts, or injuries need immediate professional attention.

😵
Seizures / Collapse

Convulsions, sudden collapse, or loss of consciousness are emergencies. Rush to a vet.

🤢
Repeated Vomiting

More than 3 times in a few hours, or vomit containing blood — don't wait, seek care.

😮‍💨
Breathing Difficulty

Laboured breathing, blue gums, or open-mouth breathing in cats is a critical emergency.

🚫
Not Eating 24+ hrs

Especially in cats and rabbits — fasting beyond 24 hrs can cause organ damage rapidly.

🌡️
High Fever

A temperature above 39.7°C (103.5°F) in dogs and cats requires prompt vet evaluation.

🐾
Can't Walk / Stand

Sudden inability to bear weight or stand could indicate spinal, neurological, or toxic causes.

☠️
Suspected Poisoning

Ingestion of chocolate, grapes, xylitol, onion, or household chemicals — emergency immediately.
